Capybara: The World's Largest Rodent


The capybara is the largest rodent in the world, native to South America. Capybaras have large bodies, weighing around 35–66 kg and reaching up to 1.3 meters in length. They have short legs and compact bodies and live in groups near water sources such as rivers, swamps, or lakes. Capybaras are herbivores that consume grass and aquatic plants. They are also known as excellent swimmers, capable of staying underwater for long periods to avoid predators.


Habitat and Distribution


Capybaras are found in various South American countries such as Brazil, Venezuela, Colombia, Argentina, and northern Uruguay. They live in areas near water sources such as rivers, swamps, lakes, or moist grasslands. As semi-aquatic animals, capybaras are often seen swimming or soaking in water to avoid predators, cool off, or search for food.
