Description : Capybara

This is the page where you can find out more about capybaras!!!



Swimming Abilities and Adaptations


One of the most remarkable abilities of the capybara is its swimming skill. Capybaras have webbed feet, which help them move quickly in the water. They can hold their breath for up to five minutes, allowing them to hide underwater from predators such as jaguars, crocodiles, or birds of prey. Additionally, capybaras rely on their ability to sneak through the water to rest or shelter from the sun's heat.


Reproduction


Capybaras breed year-round, although the rainy season is typically the peak of their mating season. After a gestation period of around 150 days, a female capybara can give birth to up to eight offspring at a time. Capybara pups are born with fur and open eyes, and they can move around and follow their mother almost immediately, as well as start eating grass within a few days after birth. Nevertheless, they will continue nursing for around 16 weeks.
